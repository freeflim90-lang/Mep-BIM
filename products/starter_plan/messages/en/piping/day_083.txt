Day 83 - Piping As-Built Requirements

1. Why This Matters
As-built piping records are critical for facilities management — valve locations, pipe diameters, and system boundaries must be accurate or maintenance and emergency isolation become guesswork.

2. Core Concept
- Common as-built changes in piping: pipe routes modified to avoid late-identified structural conflicts, valve locations adjusted for access, additional isolation valves added, pipe sizes changed due to late engineering revisions
- Most critical as-built data for piping: isolation valve locations (for emergency and maintenance isolation), pipe diameters (for flow calculations), pressure test zone boundaries
- Update process: record all RFI-approved changes in the BIM model during construction; do not batch-update at project completion
- Riser as-built accuracy: riser pipes in vertical shafts are difficult to access after construction — ensure the as-built model records the correct sleeve location and pipe offset from the shaft wall
- LOD 500: as-built piping models at LOD 500 are used as the basis for building management system integration and facilities management handover

3. Real Project Lens
On a district cooling project, the as-built piping model was issued 6 months after practical completion, reconstructed from marked-up drawings. When a chilled water isolation valve was needed for emergency maintenance 18 months later, the facilities team found that 4 valves shown in the as-built model were at different locations in the ceiling void. The building had to remain partially uncooled for 2 days while valves were located physically.

4. BIM Check
- Check: a process is in place to update the BIM model from approved RFIs and site instructions as they occur — not as a batch at the end
- Check: isolation valve locations in the as-built model are verified against physical installed positions before the model is handed over
- Check: riser pipe locations in shafts are recorded with correct offsets from the shaft walls in the as-built model

5. Common Mistake
Beginners treat as-built updating as a single end-of-project activity. By then, the details of each change have been forgotten and the update is based on incomplete records.

6. Today's Action
Think of one piping change that commonly occurs on site. Write down what information you would need from the site team to update the BIM model accurately.
