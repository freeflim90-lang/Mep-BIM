1. Why This Matters
Today marks the end of 90 days of structured MEP BIM education. The piping track has taken you from system fundamentals through to construction-issue drawings and FM handover data — the complete lifecycle of a piping BIM deliverable. More importantly, it has given you a professional vocabulary, a set of repeatable workflows, and the confidence to contribute on real MEP coordination projects.

2. Core Concept
What you have covered across 90 days:

Month 1 (Days 1–30): MEP BIM Foundations
- BIM coordination workflows, model federation, clash detection
- Discipline roles and responsibilities
- Revit essentials, coordination drawing reading

Month 2 (Days 31–60): HVAC and Mechanical Systems
- Ductwork, air systems, VAV, AHU coordination
- Mechanical room layout and equipment arrangement

Skills you now have:
- You can set up and model a complete piping system in Revit with correct system types and flow direction
- You can coordinate a mechanical room — layout, clearances, support, clash resolution
- You can produce a LOD 350-compliant piping model and coordination drawing package
- You can prepare FM-ready piping data for handover, including valve shutdown zones and equipment asset data
- You understand the full lifecycle of a piping BIM model: from design through construction to FM

What sets strong piping BIM coordinators apart:
- They understand the engineering intent — not just the model geometry
- They coordinate proactively, not reactively — services zoning before clash detection
- They build data quality habits from the start of modeling, not the end
- They communicate across disciplines clearly — the right information to the right person at the right time

3. Real Project Lens
A BIM coordinator who completed a structured 90-day piping program was brought onto a large hospital mechanical room coordination mid-project. Within two weeks, she had identified a pump connection detail that would have blocked future impeller removal, a chilled water valve with no access provision, and a missing strainer on the primary circuit return. Each finding was documented in the clash report and resolved. Her team leader's note: "She arrived already knowing what to look for. We spent time on design decisions, not on explaining coordination basics."

4. BIM Check
- Check: You can describe the end-to-end process for coordinating a mechanical plant room — from initial layout to construction-issue drawing — in five steps or fewer
- Check: You can name four FM data fields required for piping equipment handover and explain why each is needed
- Check: You can explain the coordination hierarchy for piping in a congested ceiling zone and defend the priority order

5. Common Mistake
The most common mistake at completion is treating the 90 days as finished learning. In MEP BIM, the standards, tools, and project requirements evolve continuously. The 90-day program is a foundation — not a ceiling. Apply these skills on a real project, then return to the areas where you found gaps, and deepen from there.
