Day 78 - Piping Coordination with Electrical — Separation Rules

1. Why This Matters
Piping and electrical systems share ceiling voids, risers, and plant rooms. Understanding the separation requirements between pipes and electrical equipment prevents safety hazards and coordination failures.

2. Core Concept
- Water pipes must not be routed directly above exposed live electrical equipment — including switch rooms, distribution boards, motor control centres, and server rooms
- Minimum separation: typically 300mm horizontal separation between water pipes and cable trays or conduits carrying power cables
- If pipes must cross above electrical equipment, a drip-proof tray or diverter must be installed below the pipe to catch any leaks
- Pipe flanges and joints must not be located above electrical equipment — the risk of leakage at joints is higher than at straight pipe
- In BIM: flag any location where a water pipe is within 300mm of a power cable tray or is routed above an electrical room entrance

3. Real Project Lens
On a commercial project, a chilled water pipe was routed across the ceiling of a distribution board room — with a flanged joint directly above the main busbars. The electrical engineer flagged the safety issue during the final coordination review. Re-routing the pipe required moving a 6-metre section through a congested ceiling void, adding 3 days to the piping program.

4. BIM Check
- Check: no water pipe is routed directly above an exposed live electrical distribution board, MCC, or switch room
- Check: horizontal separation between water pipes and power cable trays is at least 300mm or as specified by the project
- Check: no pipe flange or mechanical coupling is located within 600mm directly above electrical equipment

5. Common Mistake
Beginners route pipes by the shortest path without checking what is below their route. Routing above electrical equipment creates safety risks that override spatial efficiency.

6. Today's Action
Identify one location in a project or sketch where a pipe route would conflict with the electrical separation rule. Describe an alternative route that maintains the required separation.
