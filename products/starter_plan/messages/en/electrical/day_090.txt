The 10 Rules Every Electrical BIM Practitioner Must Know:
1. The distribution hierarchy (MSB → DB → circuit) must be fully modeled and named to match the SLD exactly
2. Cable tray fill: 40% maximum. HV/LV separation: 600mm parallel, 300mm crossing. LV/data: 300mm minimum
3. DB dimensions come from vendor datasheets — not generic placeholders. Front clearance (600mm minimum) must be modeled
4. Conduit type determines OD — model EMT, RGS, and PVC with correct manufacturer outer diameters
5. Pull boxes are required at 30m run length or 360° cumulative bend angle — whichever comes first
6. Every fire-rated wall/floor penetration needs a fire sleeve family with the correct fire rating parameter
7. Cable tray yields to HVAC ductwork in the routing hierarchy — tray steps, duct does not move
8. Circuit-to-load tracing must result in zero orphan circuits and zero orphan loads in the System Browser
9. LOD 350 requires accurate geometry AND complete data parameters — both must be delivered
10. FM handover data must be collected during construction — it cannot be reconstructed retrospectively

3. Real Project Lens
A junior MEP BIM practitioner completes her first coordination submission on a real project six months after completing this program. She models the electrical layer from MSB to final load, runs circuit tracing in the System Browser, resolves tray/duct clashes using the routing hierarchy, submits the fire-rated penetration schedule for structural review, and delivers the LOD 350 model with all parameters populated. Her project manager notes that no data quality issues are returned from the client's model auditor — a first for a junior team member. The 90 days of structured practice made the difference.

4. BIM Check
- Check: Can you trace any circuit in the model from the SLD → panel family → circuit in the panel schedule → connected load element — without gaps?
- Check: Does the model pass all six electrical quality gates: distribution hierarchy, tray separations, DB clearances, conduit constructibility, clash coordination, and LOD 350 parameter completeness?
- Check: Is the handover data package — COBie export or equivalent — complete and validated for all electrical elements?

5. Common Mistake
Completing 90 days of learning and then waiting for a "real project" to apply the knowledge. The transition from learning to practice requires deliberate effort: reviewing a real model, applying the checklists from this program, and seeking feedback from an experienced coordinator. Start applying the framework immediately — even on a training model — to build the habit before the first live project.
