Day 70 - Week 2 Review — Panel Board Modeling Summary

1. Why This Matters
Panel board modeling is the electrical discipline's equivalent of HVAC equipment placement — it anchors the entire distribution system in the model. Week 2 covered the DB as a physical object, as a data container, and as the origin point for every circuit in the system. This review consolidates those concepts before moving to conduit systems.

2. Core Concept

Critical Rules — Panel Board BIM
- Panel names must be consistent across all documents: the Revit family name, the panel schedule heading, and the SLD panel ID must all match exactly
- DB geometry must reflect actual vendor dimensions — not a generic placeholder
- Front clearance (600mm–1000mm depending on voltage class) must be modeled as a zone and kept clear of all other MEP elements
- Every circuit assignment requires: panel name, circuit number, phase, load classification
- High-load equipment (AHU, pump, UPS) must have dedicated single-load circuits
- Spare breaker slots must be represented as empty slots — not absent from the model
- Circuit descriptions in the model must match the engineering panel schedule word-for-word where possible
- Run the System Browser check before every coordination submission — zero orphan elements

Panel Board BIM Workflow (summary):
1. Place DB family using vendor datasheet dimensions
2. Set panel name parameter to match SLD and panel schedule
3. Model 600mm front clearance zone
4. Assign circuits from DB to all loads on the served floor or zone
5. Open panel schedule view — cross-check against engineering panel schedule
6. Confirm no orphan elements in System Browser

3. Real Project Lens
A coordination lead reviews all floor DBs before submitting the electrical model for the first coordination clash run. She checks: (1) all DB names match the SLD, (2) all DB front clearance zones are free of obstruction, (3) panel schedule views match the engineering schedules within 5%, (4) System Browser shows zero unconnected loads. This 20-minute pre-submission check has prevented three major rework cycles on past projects.

4. BIM Check
- Check: Does every DB in the model have a name that matches the engineering SLD and panel schedule exactly?
- Check: Are all panel schedule views in Revit populated — no empty schedule views for placed DB families?
- Check: Does the System Browser show zero orphan electrical elements on every floor?

5. Common Mistake
Completing the DB geometry and considering the job done — without running circuit assignments or validating the panel schedule view. The model appears complete in 3D but fails every data-quality check because the electrical intelligence layer was never built.

6. Today's Action
On your current project or training model, open the System Browser and count the number of unconnected (orphan) electrical elements. Record the number. This is your Week 2 baseline. Your target for Week 3 is zero orphans on at least one full floor.
