Day 84 - HVAC Commissioning Preparation

1. Why This Matters
Commissioning verifies that the installed HVAC system performs as designed. A well-maintained BIM model accelerates commissioning by providing accurate system data and reducing the time the commissioning team spends searching for information.

2. Core Concept
- Commissioning engineers need from the HVAC BIM model: equipment list with specifications, airflow schedule by zone and terminal, access point locations for each damper and piece of equipment
- Before commissioning: verify all equipment has manufacturer and model number in parameters, all design airflows are assigned to terminals, all access panels are modeled at their locations
- Commissioning sequence: ductwork pressure test, damper function test, fan speed verification, airflow measurement at each terminal, system balancing
- After commissioning: as-balanced airflows may differ from design — update BIM parameters if the EIR requires as-installed accuracy

3. Real Project Lens
On a large office project, the commissioning engineer requested the HVAC airflow schedule 5 days before the TAB run. The model schedule was complete and accurate, allowing the commissioning team to pre-position measurement equipment at each terminal before arriving on site. The TAB run completed in 2 days instead of 4.

4. BIM Check
- Check: all HVAC equipment in the model has a Mark value, manufacturer name, and model number in its parameters
- Check: a schedule of all supply terminals showing design airflow by zone can be exported directly from Revit
- Check: access panel locations are modeled or documented so commissioning teams can reach dampers and equipment without opening ceiling tiles randomly

5. Common Mistake
Beginners think commissioning only involves the installed system, not the model. In practice, the BIM model is the reference document for commissioning planning — an incomplete model slows commissioning.

6. Today's Action
From any HVAC model or project you know, list three pieces of information the commissioning engineer would most need to plan the TAB run.
