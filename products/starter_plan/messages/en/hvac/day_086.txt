Day 86 - Top 5 Common HVAC BIM Errors

1. Why This Matters
Learning from common errors is faster than discovering them on your own project. These five HVAC BIM errors appear repeatedly on real projects and most are completely avoidable.

2. Core Concept
- Error 1: Insulation clearance not modeled — duct looks fine but insulation was not included, causing post-coordination clashes
- Error 2: Wrong system assignment — duct modeled as exhaust is assigned to supply system, causing incorrect schedule data and missed connections in the system inspector
- Error 3: VAV box and diffuser misaligned with ceiling grid — placed for coordination convenience, not aligned to the tile pattern
- Error 4: Missing fire dampers — duct passes through fire-rated walls without damper families; only discovered during fire authority review
- Error 5: Equipment at design dimensions, not submittal dimensions — AHU or FCU modeled at the engineer's assumed size, not the approved submittal size, causing installation fit problems

3. Real Project Lens
A review of 10 HVAC models on commercial and healthcare projects found Error 4 (missing fire dampers) to be the most costly. Fire authority reviews returned models with 20 to 60 missing dampers, each requiring a model revision, coordination review, and drawing re-issue.

4. BIM Check
- Check: duct insulation clearance is included as part of the duct geometry for all specified insulated ductwork
- Check: every duct in the model is assigned to a named Revit MEP system, visible and correct in the system inspector
- Check: fire damper families are placed at every fire-rated boundary penetration before the coordination model is issued

5. Common Mistake
Beginners focus on modeling speed and miss small details like system assignment and insulation. These details take minutes to fix during modeling but hours to correct after coordination is finalized.

6. Today's Action
Review one of the five error types in any HVAC model you have access to. Can you find an example, or confirm it is not present?
