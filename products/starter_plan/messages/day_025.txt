Day 25 - Ceiling Device Coordination

1. Why This Matters
Ceilings contain diffusers, lights, sprinklers, detectors, speakers, access panels, and sometimes tight duct or pipe routes above.

2. Core Concept
Ceiling coordination aligns visible devices and hidden services so the ceiling can be built, maintained, and reviewed clearly.

3. Real Project Lens
A diffuser, sprinkler head, and light fixture may each be correct individually but conflict visually or physically when combined in the ceiling grid.

4. BIM Check
- Compare reflected ceiling plan with MEP device layout.
- Check above-ceiling service routes.
- Review access panels for valves, dampers, and equipment.

5. Common Mistake
Beginners often coordinate hidden services and visible ceiling devices separately.

6. Today's Action
Choose one small ceiling grid and list all devices that need coordinated positions.
