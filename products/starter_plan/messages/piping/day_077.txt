Day 77 - Mechanical Room Quality Check Before Submission

1. Why This Matters
The mechanical room is the most complex piping zone on any project. A dedicated quality check before submission prevents the dense pipework from hiding errors that are expensive to find during construction.

2. Core Concept
- Mechanical room quality check covers 8 areas: equipment positioning, pipe connections, valve placement, insulation clearance, maintenance access, structural coordination, fire protection, and electrical connections
- Equipment positioning: all equipment is positioned with correct maintenance clearances; lift access route is clear for the largest component
- Pipe connections: all suction and discharge connections are to the correct face of each piece of equipment; supply and return are not reversed
- Valve placement: isolation valves on both sides of every pump; commissioning valves at all terminal connections; no valve handle blocked by an adjacent pipe
- Maintenance access: there is a 900mm access corridor in front of each pump; 600mm side access to valve stems
- Structural coordination: a penetration schedule has been submitted for all slab penetrations; hanger points are on accessible structural members

3. Real Project Lens
On a hospital mechanical room, a quality check before submission identified 9 valve handles that would be blocked by adjacent pipework — the valves were physically modeled but not checked for operability. Relocating the pipes in the model took 4 hours. Relocating them after installation would have required cutting and rewelding 18 pipe joints.

4. BIM Check
- Check: all pump connections are verified against the manufacturer's connection face orientation (supply inlet on correct side)
- Check: a maintenance access corridor of 900mm exists in front of each pump and 600mm on the valve side of each major component
- Check: a penetration schedule has been submitted to the structural engineer for all slab and wall openings

5. Common Mistake
Beginners complete the mechanical room layout and submit without checking whether all valves can actually be operated in their modeled position.

6. Today's Action
Identify the 3 most critical items to check in a mechanical room BIM model before submission and explain why each matters.
