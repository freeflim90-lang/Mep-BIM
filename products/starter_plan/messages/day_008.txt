Day 8 - Cable Tray Coordination Basics

1. Why This Matters
Cable trays occupy large ceiling space and need bend radius, access, separation, and support space. They can become major coordination drivers in corridors and plant rooms.

2. Core Concept
Cable tray BIM coordination checks not only the tray body but also installation access, branching space, vertical drops, and separation from wet systems or heat sources.

3. Real Project Lens
A tray route may pass a hard clash test but still block access to dampers, valves, or ceiling panels if the corridor is crowded.

4. BIM Check
- Review tray width, bend area, and drop points.
- Check separation from piping and mechanical equipment.
- Confirm access below and beside the tray route.

5. Common Mistake
Beginners often model tray centerlines but forget the real width and bend space.

6. Today's Action
Choose one tray route and identify where a bend, drop, or access issue could happen.
