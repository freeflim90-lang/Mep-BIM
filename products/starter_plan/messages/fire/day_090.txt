The 20-Point Fire Protection BIM Checklist (Your Career Tool)
1. Correct system type per zone (wet / dry / pre-action)
2. Hazard classification per zone documented
3. Max coverage area per head verified against hazard class limit
4. Min/max head spacing verified
5. Wall offset ≤ half of max head-to-head spacing
6. 3D obstruction check completed (beams, ducts, trays)
7. Head-to-ceiling distance within specified range
8. Pipe sizes match the fire engineer's sizing schedule
9. Hose reel coverage verified by path distance (not straight-line)
10. Any pipe re-routes flagged to fire engineer
11. System configuration (tree/loop/grid) correctly modeled — loops closed
12. Head vs. duct/tray clashes resolved — no shortened drops
13. Structural beam penetrations approved and documented
14. Slab sleeve requests issued before structural drawings are finalised
15. 450 mm clear zone below deflector plate verified
16. All fire-rated penetrations have correctly sized firestop sleeves
17. Coverage verified by distribution method — not head count alone
18. Dead zones identified and remediated or documented
19. Critical path pipes identified and protected from unnotified re-routing
20. All RFIs incorporated into the model before handover issue

5. Common Mistake
Treating Day 90 as the finish line. It is not — it is the starting point for competence. The 90 days gave you the framework and the vocabulary. Real expertise comes from applying these rules across multiple projects, encountering the edge cases, making mistakes, and solving them. Keep the 20-point checklist. Use it on every project.
