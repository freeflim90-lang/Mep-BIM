Day 85 - Week 5 Review — System Verification Summary

1. Why This Matters
System verification is the quality gate between modeling and delivery. Everything modeled in Days 61–84 must be verified before the model is issued — for coordination, for construction, or for handover. Week 5 has covered the four verification activities that prevent the most serious fire protection BIM failures from reaching the field.

2. Core Concept
Week 5 Fire Protection — System Verification Summary

| Day | Topic | Key Rule |
|---|---|---|
| 81 | Verifying Head Coverage | Circle plot method — every point on the floor must be within coverage radius. Count alone is insufficient |
| 82 | Identifying Dead Zones | Dead zones occur predictably: wall offsets, post-partition areas, equipment shadows, ceiling changes |
| 83 | Pressure and Flow at Remote Head | Critical path pipes must not be re-routed, lengthened, or reduced in size without fire engineer notification |
| 84 | As-Installed vs. As-Designed | All RFIs must be incorporated into the model. As-built ≠ as-designed without documented updates |

Critical Rules — System Verification
- Coverage verification must use distribution check — not just head count
- Dead zones have predictable causes — check corners, recesses, partitioned areas, and equipment rooms
- Any coordination change on the hydraulic critical path is an engineering notification event, not a modeling decision
- As-built model must incorporate all RFIs before handover — paper RFI closure alone is not model closure
- RFI reference numbers must be stored in the modified element's parameter for traceability

Combined Running Checklist (Days 61–85)
Items 1–16: (Days 61–80 — see Day 80)
17. Coverage verified by distribution method (circle plot or equivalent) — not head count only
18. Dead zones identified and remediated or documented
19. Critical path pipes identified and protected from unnotified re-routing
20. All open RFIs incorporated into the model before issue

3. Real Project Lens
The BIM lead on a 15-storey mixed-use project applies the 20-point running checklist as a pre-issue QA gate for every fire protection model issue. Items 17–20 are the final gate before the as-built issue. The process catches a dead zone in a lobby recess (remediated), an open RFI that was resolved on paper but not in the model (updated), and a critical path pipe that had been rerouted without notification (fire engineer alerted). All three are resolved in the same day.

4. BIM Check
- Check: All 20 items on the running checklist have been reviewed for the floor or zone being issued
- Check: Coverage verification documentation (circle plot or equivalent record) is attached to the model issue
- Check: Fire engineer has been notified of and has signed off on any critical path changes made during the project

5. Common Mistake
Running verification only once — at the beginning of the coordination phase — and not rerunning it after architectural changes, RFIs, or coordination reroutes. Fire protection verification is not a one-time event. It must be repeated every time a significant change is made to the model.

6. Today's Action
Apply Items 17–20 of the running checklist to one floor of your current or sample model. Record the result for each item: pass, fail, or pending (action required). If any item fails, identify the specific element and the action needed to resolve it.
