Day 75 - Week 3 Review — Vertical Riser Summary

1. Why This Matters
Vertical risers are the spine of a multi-storey plumbing system. Days 71–75 covered the full lifecycle of a riser — from initial modeling and sizing, through the sleeve request process and shaft coordination, to the final vent-through-roof detail. This review consolidates those lessons so you can apply them as a connected workflow, not isolated steps.

2. Core Concept
Summary of Days 71–75:

| Day | Topic | Key Action |
|-----|-------|------------|
| 71 | Vertical riser basics | Model soil/waste/vent stacks as continuous pipe segments within a shaft |
| 72 | Sleeve request process | Issue slab penetration schedule with coordinates before structural reinforcement is finalized |
| 73 | Riser shaft coordination | Produce a 1:20 shaft section; separate plumbing and electrical; confirm 50 mm clearances |
| 74 | Stack vent and vent through roof | Extend stacks above roof; confirm termination location against HVAC intakes and roof plan |
| 75 | Week review | This lesson |

Riser coordination workflow in sequence:
1. Design engineer issues riser layout — confirm stack diameters and fixture unit counts
2. BIM modeler places risers in the shared shaft and produces a shaft section
3. Multi-discipline review confirms clearances and resolves overlaps
4. Sleeve request package prepared — coordinates, sizes, fire stopping requirements
5. Sleeve request issued to structural team before reinforcement cutoff date
6. Vent terminations confirmed against architectural roof plan
7. Coordination drawing issued for construction

3. Real Project Lens
A junior plumbing BIM modeler was given responsibility for the riser shaft coordination on a 12-storey residential block. By following the workflow above — starting with shaft sections, then sleeve requests, then roof vent confirmation — she issued a clean coordination drawing package on the first attempt. The senior coordinator noted that in her previous project without this workflow, the same task had required three revision cycles.

4. BIM Check
- Check: All risers in the project are modeled as continuous connected segments from basement to roof
- Check: A sleeve schedule has been produced from the Revit model and reviewed against structural drawings
- Check: Vent terminations above roof are confirmed against the architectural roof plan with clearances documented

5. Common Mistake
A common mistake is treating the sleeve request and the riser shaft coordination as separate tasks done by different team members without a shared review. When these tasks are not linked, sleeve requests are sometimes issued before shaft conflicts are resolved — meaning the sleeves are cast in the wrong location.

6. Today's Action
Sketch or model a riser coordination workflow checklist for a hypothetical 5-storey office building. Include: stack sizes, shaft section step, sleeve request step, vent extension step. Assign a responsible discipline and a timing requirement to each step. This is the format used in real BIM execution plans.
