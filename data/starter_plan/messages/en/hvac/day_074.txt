Day 74 - AHU and Mechanical Room Layout Coordination

1. Why This Matters
Mechanical rooms contain the largest HVAC equipment on a project. Getting the room layout right in BIM before construction saves rework, crane costs, and structural changes that become very expensive after concrete is poured.

2. Core Concept
- AHU room coordination involves: equipment footprint and height, maintenance clearances, vibration isolation bases, pipe connection directions, air intake and discharge routes
- Clearances: AHU typically requires 1000mm filter-access clearance on the filter face, 600mm minimum on other sides
- Structural coordination: AHU bases often require reinforced pad footings or spring isolator systems in the structural model
- Pipe connections to AHU: chilled water supply and return, condensate drain, hot water — routed to the correct connection face without blocking access
- Lift route: for equipment replacement, verify there is a lift path from the AHU to the building exterior

3. Real Project Lens
On a commercial tower, the mechanical room layout was issued without verifying lift access for AHU replacement. After construction, the facilities team discovered the AHU could not be removed without demolishing a partition wall. A $40,000 modification was required — a cost that BIM coordination would have caught before a single brick was laid.

4. BIM Check
- Check: the AHU has manufacturer-specified maintenance clearance on all sides
- Check: chilled water pipe connections are on the correct AHU face and accessible without stepping over other equipment
- Check: there is a clear lift route from the mechanical room entrance to the AHU location

5. Common Mistake
Beginners model the AHU correctly but forget the maintenance clearance zone. Placing equipment too close together looks fine on screen but fails in practice.

6. Today's Action
For one piece of HVAC equipment, find its manufacturer's minimum clearance requirements and verify whether those clearances exist in the model or sketch.
