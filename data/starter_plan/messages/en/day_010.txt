Day 10 - Why Drainage Slope Changes Coordination

1. Why This Matters
Drainage systems need slope, and slope changes vertical space. A route that looks possible in plan can fail when elevation and fall are considered.

2. Core Concept
Drainage slope means the pipe elevation changes along its route. BIM coordination must account for start elevation, end elevation, pipe size, fittings, and ceiling space.

3. Real Project Lens
A long horizontal drain may begin above the ceiling but gradually drop into a conflict zone near beams, ductwork, or cable trays.

4. BIM Check
- Confirm slope direction and slope value.
- Review start and end elevations.
- Check the route in section view along the pipe run.

5. Common Mistake
Beginners often model drainage as flat piping, then discover elevation conflicts later.

6. Today's Action
Pick one drainage route and calculate whether the end elevation will be lower than the start elevation.
