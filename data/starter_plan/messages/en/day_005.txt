Day 5 - The Beginner's Model QA Mindset

1. Why This Matters
Model QA is not a final inspection at the end. It is a daily habit that keeps the model useful for coordination, reporting, and construction discussion.

2. Core Concept
Model QA means checking whether geometry, data, view settings, naming, systems, levels, and coordination assumptions are consistent enough for the next person to trust the model.

3. Real Project Lens
A model may look acceptable in 3D, but missing levels, wrong worksets, inconsistent names, or empty parameters can make schedules and exports unreliable.

4. BIM Check
- Check geometry: location, size, elevation, and connection.
- Check data: system name, type, level, and key parameters.
- Check coordination: view filters, worksets, links, and shared coordinates.

5. Common Mistake
Beginners often treat QA as someone else's job. Strong BIM teams expect every modeler to perform small QA checks before sharing work.

6. Today's Action
Create a three-column checklist: Geometry, Data, Coordination. Add three checks under each column.

Scope note:
This is general educational content, not project review, engineering verification, code compliance confirmation, or construction approval.
