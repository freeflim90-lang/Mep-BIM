Day 13 - Using Sections to Understand Congested Areas

1. Why This Matters
Plan view can hide vertical conflicts. Sections reveal elevation, clearance, support space, and installation sequence more clearly.

2. Core Concept
A section is a vertical slice through the model. It helps you understand how elements stack above ceilings, inside shafts, and around equipment.

3. Real Project Lens
A corridor may look organized in plan but show stacked ducts, trays, pipes, and beams competing for the same vertical space in section.

4. BIM Check
- Cut sections through congested areas.
- Review top and bottom elevations of major systems.
- Compare model clearance with required installation space.

5. Common Mistake
Beginners often review coordination only in plan and 3D, skipping the most useful section views.

6. Today's Action
Create or imagine one section through a crowded ceiling zone and list the systems from top to bottom.

Scope note:
