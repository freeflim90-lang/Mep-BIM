Day 28 - Asking Better BIM Questions

1. Why This Matters
Good questions help mentors, coordinators, and engineers answer faster. Weak questions create back-and-forth and delay learning.

2. Core Concept
A strong BIM question includes context, tool, system, location, what you checked, and the decision you need.

3. Real Project Lens
Instead of saying this pipe is wrong, ask whether the chilled water return pipe at Level 02 grid C-5 should route below the tray because the current clearance is limited.

4. BIM Check
- Include system and location.
- Say what you already checked.
- Ask for a clear decision or next step.

5. Common Mistake
Beginners often ask broad questions without context, making it hard to give practical guidance.

6. Today's Action
Rewrite one broad BIM question into a specific question with context and desired decision.

Scope note:
This is general educational content, not project review, engineering verification, code compliance confirmation, or construction approval.
