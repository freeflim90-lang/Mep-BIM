Day 88 - HVAC Deep-Dive Wrap-Up

1. Why This Matters
Day 88 completes the HVAC discipline deep-dive. This is a moment to consolidate what you have covered, identify what to practice next, and prepare for what real HVAC BIM work looks like in the field.

2. Core Concept
- Over the past 28 days you have covered: system flow from AHU to diffuser, duct sizing logic, VAV systems, AHU room layout, fire dampers, condensate drainage, smoke control, submittals, LOD 350, quality checks, commissioning support, and as-built requirements
- Key competencies you have built: reading HVAC systems in Revit, identifying coordination conflicts between HVAC and other trades, applying model quality standards, understanding commissioning and as-built processes
- What comes next in practice: these concepts are entry-level competencies for an HVAC BIM modeler — the next step is applying them on a real project or a practice model
- Areas to keep developing: duct sizing calculations, Navisworks clash grouping, system balancing documentation, and working with MEP engineers on design intent

3. Real Project Lens
A junior HVAC BIM modeler who completed a structured 90-day program joined a hospital project team. In the first week, they identified a missing fire damper that the senior coordinator had overlooked during a busy review period. The find was small, but it demonstrated coordination awareness beyond just drawing ducts — and the junior modeler was included in coordination meetings earlier than expected.

4. BIM Check
- Check: you can explain what a VAV system is and why VAV boxes require ceiling access panels
- Check: you can name the five most common HVAC BIM errors and describe how to check for each one
- Check: you can describe what information the commissioning engineer needs from the HVAC BIM model before a TAB run

5. Common Mistake
Beginners sometimes finish a training program feeling they are not ready for real work. The foundation is real. The next step is practice, not more theory.
