Day 73 - Pipe Insulation — Specification, Clearance, and Coordination

1. Why This Matters
Pipe insulation serves multiple purposes: preventing heat gain, preventing condensation, and providing acoustic damping. In BIM, insulation must be modeled as an additional clearance layer — not just noted on a drawing.

2. Core Concept
- Chilled water pipes require insulation to prevent condensation and heat gain — typical thickness is 25-50mm depending on pipe size and ambient conditions
- Insulation adds to the effective diameter of the pipe — a 100mm pipe with 38mm insulation has an overall diameter of 176mm
- In Revit: insulation can be applied to pipes as an additional layer using the pipe insulation parameter — this adds to the model geometry for clash detection
- Coordination impact: insulated pipes require more clearance from walls, slabs, structural beams, and other services
- Insulation type: for chilled water, typically closed-cell foam (Armaflex or similar) — this differs from the mineral wool used on hot water and steam pipes

3. Real Project Lens
On a commercial building, pipe insulation was not modeled in the BIM coordination. After installation, 12 insulated chilled water pipes were found to be touching or pressing against structural beams — creating thermal bridges that caused condensation and dripping. The contractor had to re-route or re-insulate all 12 locations.

4. BIM Check
- Check: pipe insulation thickness is applied in the Revit pipe insulation parameter for all chilled water and hot water pipes
- Check: insulated pipe diameter (pipe plus insulation) is used for all clash detection runs, not bare pipe diameter
- Check: the minimum clearance between insulated pipes and adjacent surfaces meets the specification requirement

5. Common Mistake
Beginners model bare pipe sizes and assume installers will add insulation without coordination. Insulated pipes are significantly larger and require dedicated clearance space in the ceiling void.

6. Today's Action
For one chilled water pipe in a model or sketch, calculate the total installed diameter including insulation. How much more clearance does this require compared to the bare pipe?
