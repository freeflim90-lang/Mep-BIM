Day 69 - Circuit Tracing in the Model — Connecting Loads Back to Panels in Revit

1. Why This Matters
Circuit tracing means following the electrical path from a panel breaker to the equipment it feeds, inside the Revit model. This connection is what makes a BIM model electrically intelligent — it enables panel schedule generation, load calculation, and system verification. Junior modelers who understand circuit tracing can validate model completeness and support design coordination.

2. Core Concept
In Revit, an electrical circuit is a logical connection between a source (panel/DB) and one or more loads (light fixtures, sockets, equipment).

Steps for circuit tracing and assignment:
- Select the load element (e.g., a lighting fixture or mechanical equipment)
- Use Modify > Power (or Electrical > Power) to create a power circuit
- Assign the circuit to the correct panel by selecting from the panel list
- Specify the phase, circuit number, and wire size if required by project standards
- Repeat for all loads on the floor or zone
- Open the panel schedule view to verify the circuit appears correctly

Circuit tracing verification:
- Use Revit's System Browser to view all circuits on a panel
- Check that no load is unconnected (orphan elements show no circuit assignment)
- Confirm that loads are connected to the correct floor's DB — a Level 3 light should not be on DB-L4
- Check that high-load equipment (AHUs, pumps) have dedicated circuits, not shared circuits
- Verify that circuit descriptions in the model match the engineering panel schedule

Common Revit parameters to check: Panel Name, Circuit Number, Load Classification, Apparent Load (VA), Number of Poles

3. Real Project Lens
A BIM coordinator is asked to verify that all lighting fixtures on Level 6 are connected to DB-L6. She opens the System Browser, filters by DB-L6, and lists all connected loads. She finds 47 fixtures — but the reflected ceiling plan shows 52. The five missing fixtures are orphan elements placed but never circuit-assigned. She flags these for the electrical modeler to connect before the next coordination submission.

4. BIM Check
- Check: Are all electrical load elements (lights, sockets, equipment) assigned to a circuit in the System Browser — with no orphan (unconnected) elements?
- Check: Does each circuit connect to the correct floor DB — not a DB on a different floor or an MSB directly?
- Check: Do dedicated high-load circuits (HVAC equipment, lifts, server room UPS) appear as single-load circuits, not shared with general power?

5. Common Mistake
Placing equipment families in the model but never running the circuit assignment step. The model looks complete visually, but the panel schedule remains empty or incomplete — making it useless for load verification or handover documentation.

6. Today's Action
In Revit (or a training model), select three light fixtures on the same floor. Assign all three to a single circuit connected to a panel. Open the panel schedule view and confirm the circuit appears with the correct load. Check the System Browser to see the circuit listed.
