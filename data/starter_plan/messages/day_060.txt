Day 60 - Foundation Complete: What You Now Know

1. Why This Matters
Day 60 marks the end of the Foundation curriculum. Before moving into your discipline deep-dive, it is worth reviewing what you have built — because the value of a foundation is not obvious until you start applying it under pressure.

2. Core Concept
You have covered 8 tracks over 60 days. Here is what each one gives you in practice:

- Track 1 (MEP BIM Orientation): You understand why BIM exists and what coordination is trying to achieve.
- Track 2 (Revit MEP Basics): You can navigate Revit MEP, understand worksets, and read model structure.
- Track 3 (Drawing and System Reading): You can read MEP drawings, identify system flows, and connect drawings to the model.
- Track 4 (Model Quality Basics): You know what LOD means, what parameters matter, and how to self-check your work.
- Track 5 (Clash Coordination Basics): You understand hard and soft clashes, Navisworks workflows, and coordination meeting structure.
- Track 6 (Data and Schedule Basics): You can read and audit Revit schedules, and understand data export and change tracking.
- Track 7 (Site-Readiness Thinking): You understand how BIM connects to site installation and what construction teams need.
- Track 8 (BIM Career Habit Building): You have a framework for how to behave and grow on a real BIM team.

3. Real Project Lens
A junior modeler with this foundation can walk into a coordination meeting, understand the clash discussion, identify which model element is involved, and know what questions to ask. That is a fundamentally different starting point than someone who has only used Revit in isolation.

4. BIM Check
- Check: Can you explain what a BIM Execution Plan is and what it governs?
- Check: Can you describe the five steps in a basic clash coordination workflow?
- Check: Can you name three things a site supervisor needs from the BIM team?

5. Common Mistake
Learners at this stage sometimes underestimate their foundation because they have not applied it to a real project yet. The foundation is real — application comes with practice.
