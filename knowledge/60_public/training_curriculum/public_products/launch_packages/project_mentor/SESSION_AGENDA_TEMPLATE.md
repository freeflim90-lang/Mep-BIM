## PRE-SESSION PREPARATION CHECKLIST

### For the Client (sent 48 hours before the session)

Send this message 48 hours before the scheduled Zoom:

> "Your monthly session is in 2 days — [Date] at [Time] [Time Zone]. To make the most of our 60 minutes, please prepare the following:
>
> 1. **Your top project challenge this month.** What is the one thing that is most occupying your mind on your current project right now? Coordination issue, BEP gap, team management difficulty, client communication challenge — anything. Be ready to describe it in 3–5 minutes.
>
> 2. **Your top question.** The one thing you most want to walk away with clarity on.
>
> 3. **Action item review.** Look at the action items from last month's session summary. Which are complete? Which are still open, and why?
>
> 4. **Any document or topic you want to review.** If there's a section of your BEP, a QA/QC checklist approach, a delivery plan, or a team communication issue you want to talk through, let me know in advance and I'll prepare.
>
> 5. **Career goal check.** One sentence: are you closer to your career goal than you were 30 days ago? What moved, what stalled?
>
> Zoom link: [link]
> Duration: 60 minutes. We start on time."

### For the Mentor (internal, complete before session)

- [ ] Review client CRM record: career goal, package type, start date, previous session notes
- [ ] Review all Telegram exchanges from the past 30 days — note recurring themes, questions, challenges
- [ ] Review previous session's post-session summary and check action item completion rate
- [ ] Check whether a delivery simulation was issued this month — if so, review the client's submission and feedback
- [ ] Check quarterly assessment due date — if within 30 days, prepare to raise this in Section 5
- [ ] Assess career goal trajectory: is the client progressing? Any gaps emerging?
- [ ] Prepare 2–3 proactive discussion prompts based on the client's specific situation
- [ ] Confirm the Zoom link is working and recording (if the client consented)
- [ ] Set a 55-minute timer to close gracefully at 60 minutes

## SESSION STRUCTURE

### Section 1: Opening and Monthly Orientation (0:00 – 5:00)
**Duration: 5 minutes**

**Purpose:** Confirm the session agenda, check the client's mindset, set the tone for a productive 60 minutes.

**Mentor script guide:**
- Welcome the client. Note anything specific from your review that shows you were paying attention: "I saw your message about the structural revision situation on Wednesday — we can dig into that today."
- Confirm the session agenda: "We have 60 minutes. Here's what I'm thinking: we'll start with your main challenge or question, then go into [specific topic based on intake prep], cover your action items from last month, and close with a career check-in. Does that work, or do you want to move things around?"
- Allow the client to adjust the agenda. Their priorities lead.

**Notes box:**
```
Client stated priorities for this session: ___________________
Adjustments to planned agenda: ____________________________
Client mood / engagement: _________________________________
```

---

### Section 2: Current Project Challenge and Expert Q&A (5:00 – 25:00)
**Duration: 20 minutes**

**Purpose:** Address the real, pressing challenges the client is dealing with. This is the highest-value section of the call and should always receive the most time.

**Protocol:**
1. Ask the client to describe their main challenge (3–5 minutes for them to explain).
2. Ask at least 2 clarifying questions before offering a response. Do not jump to answers — understand the full picture first.
3. Provide structured expert guidance (10–12 minutes). For complex issues, use a framework: "Here's the principle, here's how I'd apply it in your situation, here's what I'd do first."
4. Reference applicable standards (ISO 19650, BEP best practices, QA/QC frameworks) where relevant.
5. Check understanding: "Does that give you what you need, or do you want to go deeper on any part?"

**If the client has multiple questions:** Acknowledge all at the start. Prioritize with the client. Defer lower-priority questions to Telegram or the next session — do not rush through all of them and deliver shallow answers.

**Notes box:**
```
Client's main challenge described: __________________________
Clarifying questions asked: ________________________________
Key guidance provided: ____________________________________
Standards or frameworks referenced: _______________________
Follow-up needed: Yes / No
```

### Section 3: Document or System Development (25:00 – 40:00)
**Duration: 15 minutes**

**Purpose:** Work on a specific BIM Lead deliverable or system. This section has rotating focus depending on the client's development stage:

- **Month 1–2:** BEP structure and writing guidance (walk through sections together)
- **Month 2–4:** QA/QC system development (review checklists, discuss application)
- **Month 4–6:** Delivery strategy and stakeholder management
- **Month 6+:** Advanced leadership topics (team management, subcontractor accountability, client reporting)

Adjust the focus based on the client's intake goals and current progression. The mentor decides the focus for each session and proposes it at the start of Section 1.

**Protocol:**
1. Introduce the topic: "Today I want to use this 15 minutes to work through [specific section of BEP / QA checklist / delivery plan]. Let's look at what you have and build on it."
2. If the client has a document or draft to share: ask them to screen-share or paste the relevant section into Telegram before the call.
3. Provide specific, directive feedback. Be direct — this is a mentoring session, not a polite review. If something is wrong, say so clearly and explain why.
4. End with a specific writing or system task for the client to complete before the next session.

**Topic options by stage (select one per session):**
- BEP: Section writing (propose drafting Section 2 — Roles and Responsibilities)
- BEP: Information requirements (defining LOD by stage)
- BEP: CDE structure and naming convention
- QA/QC: Adapting the checklist to the client's actual project discipline
- QA/QC: Implementing a review gate process within the team
- Coordination process: Designing a clash review meeting workflow
- Delivery: Building a pre-submission verification checklist
- Reporting: Structuring a progress report to a main contractor

**Notes box:**
```

Key feedback given: _____________________________________
Task assigned: _________________________________________
```

### Section 4: Action Item Review and Goal Setting (40:00 – 52:00)
**Duration: 12 minutes**

**Purpose:** Accountability. Close the loop on last month's commitments. Set new, specific commitments for the coming month.

**Protocol:**
1. Pull up the action items from the previous post-session summary.
2. For each item: Completed / In Progress / Not Started.
3. For Not Started items: ask why, not as a criticism but as a diagnostic. Time? Clarity? Scope too large? Adjust accordingly.
4. Set 3–5 new action items for the coming month. These must be:
   - Specific (not "work on the BEP" — "complete Section 3 of the BEP using the template")
   - Achievable in the timeframe (realistic given the client's workload)
   - Verifiable (the client can confirm they did it; LUA BIM LABS can check at next session)

### Section 5: Career Trajectory Check-In (52:00 – 58:00)
**Duration: 6 minutes**

**Purpose:** Maintain focus on the big picture. The monthly Zoom is not just about solving this month's problems — it is about building toward a career goal.

**Protocol:**
1. Ask: "Stepping back — are you closer to [their stated career goal] than you were 30 days ago? What moved?"
2. Acknowledge what is going well — specifically. Do not offer generic encouragement.
3. Identify one area where the client's growth is slower than expected or where a gap is appearing.
4. If quarterly assessment is due in the next 30 days: mention it now. "I'll be sending you the quarterly assessment form at the end of this month. It takes about 20 minutes. We'll use the results to build your Career Growth Report together."


### Section 6: Close and Next Steps (58:00 – 60:00)
**Duration: 2 minutes**

**Purpose:** Clean, confident close. The client leaves the call knowing exactly what comes next.

**Protocol:**
1. Summarize in 2 sentences: "Here's what we covered today: [brief summary]."
2. Confirm action items: "You have [N] action items before next session — they'll be in the summary I send tonight."
3. Next session: "I'll send your next scheduling link at the start of [next month]."
4. Delivery simulation reminder (if applicable): "Keep an eye on Telegram — I'll send your delivery simulation scenario this week."
5. Close genuinely: one specific, earned sentence of acknowledgement.
6. End the call on time — or 30 seconds before.

**Notes box:**
```
Session ended at: _____ (target: 60 minutes)
Client sentiment at close: Energized / Satisfied / Neutral / Overwhelmed
Any immediate follow-up needed: _______________________
```

## RECORDING AND NOTE POLICY

**Recording:**
- Zoom sessions may be recorded with the client's explicit consent, given at intake or at the start of the session.
- If the client consents: record via Zoom. Store the recording in the client's folder on LUA BIM LABS Google Drive (access restricted to mentors and COO). Retention: 90 days.
- If the client does not consent: do not record. Rely on mentor's session notes.

**Note-taking:**
- Mentors must complete all "Notes box" fields in this template during the session.
- Notes are transferred to the CRM Session Notes field within 24 hours of the session.
- Notes are the internal record. The Post-Session Summary is the client-facing record. They are not the same document.

## RESCHEDULING POLICY

### Client-Initiated Reschedule
- 48+ hours notice: reschedule at no penalty within the same calendar month. Send Calendly link.
- Less than 48 hours notice: session counts as used for that month.
- No-show: session counts as used. Send the No-Show Message (PM-NS01) within 2 hours.

**PM-NS01: No-Show Message**
> "Hi [Name], I was in the Zoom room for our session today at [time]. No problem — things come up. This will count as your session for the month. I'll send the scheduling link for next month's session in the first week of [next month]. If you'd like to cover anything in the meantime, I'm here on Telegram."

### LUA BIM LABS-Initiated Reschedule
- Notify the client at least 72 hours in advance.
- Provide 3 alternative time slots within the same calendar month.
- If no suitable alternatives: offer a 75-minute session the following month (the only exception to the 60-minute cap).

### Intensive Package Sessions (2 per month)
- Same policies apply to each session individually.
- If both sessions are missed in one month: one may be offered as a makeup in the following month, at LUA BIM LABS' discretion.
