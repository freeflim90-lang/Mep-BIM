This assessment takes approximately 20–25 minutes to complete honestly. There are no right or wrong answers. The purpose is to capture where you are right now — not where you want to be or where you think you should be.

Your responses are used by LUA BIM LABS to:
1. Generate your Career Growth Report (delivered within 5 business days of your submission)
2. Focus your next mentoring session on the areas where you most need development
3. Track your growth over time (your first assessment is the baseline; future assessments measure change)

**Be honest.** A low self-score is not a failure — it is useful information. An inflated score helps no one.

## SCORING SYSTEM

### Part 1: Competency Score Calculation

Record each client's scores in the CRM. Calculate:

| Metric | Formula |
|--------|---------|
| Total Score | Sum of all 15 scores (maximum: 75) |
| Technical Score | Sum of scores 1–8 (maximum: 40) |
| Leadership Score | Sum of scores 9–12 (maximum: 20) |
| Strategic Score | Sum of scores 13–15 (maximum: 15) |
| Average Score | Total Score ÷ 15 |

**Benchmark interpretation by career stage:**

| Career Stage | Expected Average | Expected Technical | Expected Leadership | Expected Strategic |
|-------------|-----------------|-------------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Junior BIM Lead (< 2 yrs in role) | 2.0–2.8 | 2.5–3.5 | 1.5–2.5 | 1.0–2.0 |
| Mid BIM Lead (2–5 yrs in role) | 2.8–3.5 | 3.0–4.0 | 2.5–3.5 | 2.0–3.0 |
| Senior BIM Lead (5+ yrs in role) | 3.5–4.5 | 3.5–4.5 | 3.0–4.5 | 2.5–4.0 |

**Flags to note:**
- Technical score significantly higher than Leadership: client may be stuck in an individual contributor mindset, limiting career progression
- Leadership score higher than Technical: possible confidence issue on the technical side, or genuinely needs to deepen BIM knowledge base
- Strategic score below 2.0 for clients targeting PM or consulting roles: this is a significant gap; prioritize this in the next quarter
- Any individual score of 1 (especially in Technical competencies 1–7): likely a priority mentoring focus area

### Quarter-on-Quarter Growth Tracking

For clients on their 2nd or subsequent assessment:

| Growth Rate | Interpretation |
|------------|---------------|
| +0.5 or more per competency (avg) | Strong growth — mentoring is working |
| +0.2 to +0.4 average | Steady growth — expected for most |
| 0 to +0.2 average | Slow growth — investigate: is the client applying learning? Are action items being completed? |
| No change or decline | Concerning — initiate a direct conversation about commitment and alignment |

## INTERPRETING PART 2: OPEN QUESTIONS

Read Part 2 responses looking for:

**In Q2.1 (biggest challenge):** Does the challenge align with a competency gap? If a client scores low on subcontractor management (C10) and their biggest challenge is a subcontractor not delivering models, that's a clear alignment. The challenge can be addressed directly through mentoring.

**In Q2.2 (most proud of):** Does the accomplishment reflect growth from earlier in the program? If a first-assessment client says "I finished a coordination meeting without my manager needing to step in," note it. At assessment 2, look for evidence that confidence has grown.

**In Q2.3 and Q2.4 (least confident / situation handled poorly):** These are the gold. Clients often reveal the actual gap here that the competency scores obscure. If a client scores themselves a 3 on BEP authoring but Q2.4 reveals they failed to write a BEP section correctly under a deadline, note the discrepancy — the real level may be closer to 2.

**In Q2.5 (team and stakeholder relationships):** Look for recurring relationship issues — particularly repeated difficulty with subcontractor accountability or difficulty communicating with non-BIM stakeholders. These are leadership development themes.

---

## INTERPRETING PART 3: CAREER GOAL QUESTIONS

**Q3.1 — Career goal alignment:**
- If the stated goal has changed since intake, note this in CRM and discuss in the next Zoom session. Career goal drift is common and not a problem — but the mentoring program focus may need to be adjusted.
- If the client is still "figuring it out" after 90 days, initiate a structured career goal conversation in the next session. Vagueness here often reflects fear of commitment rather than genuine uncertainty.

**Q3.3 — Biggest obstacle:**
Common honest answers: skills gap, lack of seniority at current company, imposter syndrome, financial need to stay in the current role, family constraints on relocation. Each of these has a different mentoring response. Do not give the same advice for "I lack the skills" as for "I have the skills but no one is giving me a chance."

**Q3.4 — Self-identified gaps:**
Cross-reference against the Part 1 scores. If the client identifies the same competencies in Q3.4 as they scored lowest in Part 1, that's good self-awareness — confirm the alignment in the report. If there's a mismatch (e.g., client scores themselves 4 on BEP authoring but identifies BEP as a gap), probe this in the next session.

## INTERNAL NOTES — COMMON PATTERNS AND RESPONSES

**Pattern: Client consistently scores leadership competencies low, but their career goal is BIM Director.**
Response: This is the most common progression blocker. The priority for the next quarter is leadership — not technical depth. Recommend specific experiences: "Can you take on the role of leading one subcontractor coordination meeting this month and report back?"

**Pattern: Client's scores have not changed from Quarter 1 to Quarter 2.**
Response: This almost always means action items are not being completed, or the client is not applying mentoring insights to real situations. Raise this directly in the Zoom session. "Your scores haven't moved in 90 days. That's not a judgment — but I want to understand what's getting in the way. What happened with [specific action item]?"

**Pattern: Client scores themselves high across the board (average 4+) on the first assessment.**
Response: This is often self-deception or social performance (appearing competent). Probe this in the Zoom session with direct scenario questions: "Walk me through how you'd structure a BEP for a hospital project with 6 MEP subcontractors." If the response is vague or incomplete, note the gap gently and adjust the score internally for your own planning. Do not directly contradict the self-score in the report — instead, design the mentoring to expose the real level.

**Pattern: Client's open questions reveal imposter syndrome (feels like a fraud at the BIM Lead level).**
Response: This is common and treatable. Acknowledge it explicitly in the report: "One thing I noticed in your answers: you hold yourself to a high standard, sometimes to a point where you underestimate what you actually know. [Specific example from their answers.] That's worth naming." Then provide a reframe: competence gaps are real and addressable, but they don't make you an imposter — they make you someone who hasn't learned something yet.
