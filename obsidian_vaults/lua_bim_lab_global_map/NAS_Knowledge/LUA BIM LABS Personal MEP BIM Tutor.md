# LUA BIM LABS Personal MEP BIM Tutor

## Strategic Position

LUA BIM LABS should position the Telegram education product as personalized 1:1 MEP BIM tutoring, not as a generic daily BIM tip service.

The strongest promise is:

> Daily MEP BIM lessons tailored to your current level, discipline, weak points, and project goals.

## Why This Positioning Works

- It supports premium pricing better than a generic content subscription.
- It fits Telegram because conversation, feedback, and daily habit-building are natural there.
- It can serve beginners, Revit MEP modelers, coordinators, leads, and automation-focused learners.
- It creates reusable knowledge assets when Q&A and lesson insights are captured in Obsidian.
- It connects the public blog funnel to a paid personal mentoring service.

## Required Client Profile

Each client should have a profile with:

- BIM level
- MEP discipline
- Revit MEP experience
- Navisworks experience
- project type experience
- goal
- weak points
- preferred lesson language
- daily study time

## Level-Based Lesson Adaptation

One topic should branch by learner level.

Example topic: insulation thickness in clash coordination.

- Beginner: what insulation is and why it changes real space.
- Modeler: how to model and check insulation in Revit MEP.
- Coordinator: how to separate hard clashes and clearance issues.
- Lead: how to include insulation rules in BEP and QA standards.
- Automation learner: how to detect missing insulation with Dynamo or Revit API.

## Obsidian Capture Rule

When a Telegram lesson or client Q&A contains reusable MEP BIM insight, capture it as:

- knowledge insight
- educational insight
- practical case
- checklist
- reuse idea

This keeps the tutoring service from being only a conversation and turns it into a growing LUA BIM LABS education asset.
